Problem Title: Pour Water Between Buckets to Make Water Levels Equal
Brute Force Method: Simulate pouring water drop by drop from the fullest bucket to the emptiest until they balance.
BF Complexity Visuals: An infinite loop of tiny subtractions and additions.
How to Draw BF: Draw buckets. Erase one drop from a full bucket, draw it in an empty bucket, subtract the spill percentage. Repeat forever. $O(\text{WaterAmount})$.
Optimised Pattern: Binary Search on Answer.
Opt Vis. Technique: A horizontal laser sweeping up and down to find the perfect equilibrium line.
Dry Run (Opt): Guess a target water level `mid`. Calculate total water spilled to bring high buckets down to `mid`. Check if remaining water is enough to bring low buckets up to `mid`. Adjust `low` and `high` bounds.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw an uneven bar chart. Draw a horizontal line across the middle. Shade the tops of bars above the line (water to pour) and the empty space below the line (water needed). Balance the shaded areas.
Opt Complexity Visuals: $O(N \log(\text{Max} - \text{Min})$) search space.
How to Draw Opt Complexity: A binary search tree curve shrinking down to a single horizontal float value.
Mem Vis (BF): Deep iteration counters.
Mem Vis (Opt): $O(1)$ extra space. Just a few float variables.
