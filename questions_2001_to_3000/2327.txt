Problem Title: Number of People Aware of a Secret
Brute Force Method: Maintain a giant list of every individual person. Each day, loop through the entire list to update their "days known" and append new people if they fall in the sharing window.
BF Complexity Visuals: Exponential Population List (O(2^N))
How to Draw BF: Draw a list of stick figures that doubles or triples in size every few steps, becoming too massive to count.
Optimised Pattern: 1D Dynamic Programming / Sliding Window
Opt Vis. Technique: Sliding "Active Sharer" Window
Dry Run (Opt): Use a DP array where `dp[i]` is the number of new people who found out on day `i`. The number of new people on day `i` is the sum of `dp[i - forget]` to `dp[i - delay]`. Keep a running window sum to avoid re-looping.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw a timeline of days. Above the timeline, draw a rectangular bracket representing the "sharing window" (from delay to forget). As days pass, slide the bracket right, summing the numbers inside it to generate the next day's value.
Opt Complexity Visuals: Linear Window Slide (O(N))
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a timeline. Draw a fixed-width block sliding smoothly from left to right along the timeline.
Mem Vis (BF): Draw a massive, dynamically expanding Array simulating individual people.
Mem Vis (Opt): Draw a fixed-size Circular Queue or a 1D DP Array of size N, storing counts instead of individuals.
