Problem Title: Total Sales Amount by Year (SQL)
Brute Force Method: For every sale, calculate the duration, then check every day in the calendar to see which year it belongs to, summing manually.
BF Complexity Visuals: $O(\text{Sales} \\text{cdot Days})$ Nested Logic
How to Draw BF: Draw a sale spanning 3 years. Draw an arrow for every single day in that range, checking the year. Massive CPU overhead for date ranges.
Optimised Pattern: Recursive CTE (Year-Level Pivot)
Opt Vis. Technique: Yearly Segment Slicing
Dry Run (Opt): Use a Recursive CTE to generate the years 2018, 2019, 2020. Join with Sales where the year is between `start_date` and `end_date`. Calculate overlap days per year.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw a sale bar. Place three boxes representing 2018, 2019, and 2020. Cut the sale bar into parts that fit into each box. Multiply each part by the daily price.
Opt Complexity Visuals: $O(\text{Sales} \cdot 3)$ Linearized Split
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a sale record entering a machine that splits it into at most 3 rows (one for each valid year).
Mem Vis (BF): Massive Daily Logs: Creating a row for every single day in a 3-year period in memory.
Mem Vis (Opt): 3-Row Splitter: Draw a structure where one input row transforms into a maximum of 3 output rows.
