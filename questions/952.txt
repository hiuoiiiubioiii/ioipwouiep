Problem Title: Largest Component Size by Common Factor
Brute Force Method: For every pair of numbers (a, b), calculate GCD(a, b). If &gt; 1, draw an edge between them. Finally, find the largest connected component.
BF Complexity Visuals: $O(N^2 \cdot \log(\text{maxVal})$). Quadratic pair-checks with logarithmic GCD math.
How to Draw BF: Draw dots for numbers. Draw lines between every single pair that shares a factor. The paper becomes a dense, unreadable cobweb.
Optimised Pattern: Union-Find + Prime Factorization
Opt Vis. Technique: "The Prime Hub." Instead of connecting numbers directly, connect each number to its prime factors. Numbers sharing a prime "hub" are automatically joined.
Dry Run (Opt): For each `num`, find its prime factors (up to √num). Union `num` with all its prime factors. Use a map to count which parent has the most children.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw numbers on top and prime numbers (2, 3, 5...) in the middle. Draw lines from numbers to their factors. Numbers that "plug into" the same prime are one group.
Opt Complexity Visuals: $O(N \cdot √\text{maxVal})$. Much faster than checking all pairs.
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a bipartite graph mapping Numbers to Prime Factors. Use Union-Find to merge the "Prime" sets.
Mem Vis (BF): $O(N^2)$ memory to store all edges in the graph.
Mem Vis (Opt): $O(\text{maxVal})$ for the Union-Find parent array. Draw a single long row of boxes.
