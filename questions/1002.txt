ID: 1002
Problem Title: Find Common Characters
Brute Force Method: Pick the first word. For each of its characters, check if it exists in every other word. If yes, remove it from all words and add to the result.
BF Complexity Visuals: $O(N \cdot L^2)$. Repeatedly searching and modifying strings (which is expensive).
How to Draw BF: Draw three words. Pick 'a' from word 1. Hunt for 'a' in word 2 and 3. Erase them with a pencil. Repeat. The paper is messy with erasures.
Optimised Pattern: Frequency Intersection (Min-Tally)
Opt Vis. Technique: "The Lowest Common Denominator." Count the characters in the first word. For every subsequent word, update your count to be the *minimum* of what you currently have and what the new word has.
Dry Run (Opt): 1. `minFreq` = 26-slot array for word[0]. 2. For word in words[1:]: get `currFreq`. For `i` in 0..25: `minFreq[i] = min(minFreq[i], currFreq[i])`.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw a 26-slot "Master Frequency" bar chart. For each new word, "squash" the bars down so they never exceed the height of the current word's bars.
Opt Complexity Visuals: $O(N \cdot L)$. You look at every character in every word exactly once.
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a vertical stack of 26-slot arrays. Draw an arrow pointing down through each slot, taking the `min` value to the next row.
Mem Vis (BF): $O(N \cdot L)$ for storing modified versions of the words.
Mem Vis (Opt): $O(1)$ extra space (fixed 26-size arrays). Draw two rows of 26 boxes: `Master` and `Current`.
