Problem Title: Pancake Sorting
Brute Force Method: Generate all possible sequences of "pancake flips" and for each, check if the resulting array is sorted.
BF Complexity Visuals: $O(N!)$ or higher. Exploring an infinite number of flip combinations.
How to Draw BF: Draw a stack of pancakes. Draw 5 different ways to flip it. For each, draw 5 more. Show a massive tree of "shuffled" pancake stacks.
Optimised Pattern: Greedy "Largest to Back"
Opt Vis. Technique: "The Spatula Slide." Find the largest unsorted pancake. Flip it to the top (index 1), then flip the whole stack to move it to its correct position at the bottom.
Dry Run (Opt): For `curr_size` from N down to 1: find index of `curr_size`. Flip at `index + 1` (moves to front). Flip at `curr_size` (moves to back). Repeat.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw a stack of pancakes of different widths. Show a spatula sliding under the widest one, flipping the top part, and then flipping the whole stack to put the wide one on the plate.
Opt Complexity Visuals: $O(N^2)$. We perform two flips for each of the N pancakes.
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw the array. Below it, draw a "Flip" icon and the resulting array. Repeat this N times, with each array having one more "sorted" element at the end.
Mem Vis (BF): $O(N!)$ to store permutations.
Mem Vis (Opt): $O(N)$ to store the list of flip indices. Draw a single row of "Flip" integers.
