Problem Title: Time to Cross a Bridge
Brute Force Method: Simulate every single second of time. At each second, check the status of all 4 potential queues and the bridge, moving workers one step at a time. $O(\text{Time} \cdot N)$.
BF Complexity Visuals: A granular timeline where every millisecond is a discrete state change for all workers.
How to Draw BF: Draw a timeline. For every tick mark, redraw the bridge and the position of every worker. This becomes unmanageable as time increases.
Optimised Pattern: Event-Driven Simulation using 4 Priority Queues (Heaps).
Opt Vis. Technique: A "State Machine" where workers jump between four priority heaps based on their readiness and efficiency scores.
Dry Run (Opt): Manage workers in heaps: leftWait, rightWait (sorted by efficiency), leftFree, rightFree (sorted by time). Always prioritize rightWait crossing left. Jump time to the next available event (worker finishing a task).
How to Draw (Opt): Draw a bridge. On each side, draw two boxes (Waiting and Working). Use arrows to move worker IDs between boxes. Always pick the worker from the "Waiting" box with the highest efficiency value.
Opt Complexity Visuals: $O(N \log W)$ where N is boxes and W is workers. Each worker movement is a heap operation.
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw the bridge. Draw 4 triangles (heaps). Show worker IDs moving from one triangle to the next. Annotate each move with a log W cost.
Mem Vis (BF): A massive array or list tracking every worker's position for every second of time.
Mem Vis (Opt): Exactly 4 Heaps storing worker objects/tuples.
