ID: 556
Problem Title: Next Greater Element III
Brute Force Method: Generate all permutations of the digits of the given number, sort them, and find the one immediately larger than the input.
BF Complexity Visuals: Factorial growth of digit permutations. $O(D!)$.
How to Draw BF: Draw the number (e.g., 123). Draw a tree branching into 132, 213, 231, 312, 321. Circle the one just above 123.
Optimised Pattern: Next Permutation Algorithm (Linear Scan).
Opt Vis. Technique: A mountain range. You walk from right-to-left looking for the first "dip" (decrease), then swap and reverse the "slope" to make it as small as possible.
Dry Run (Opt): 1. Find first digit `i` from right where `arr[i] &lt; arr[i+1]`. 2. Find digit `j` from right where `arr[j] &gt; arr[i]`. 3. Swap `i` and `j`. 4. Reverse everything after index `i`.
How to Draw (Opt): Write the digits (e.g., 1 5 8 4 7 6 5 3 1). Draw a left-arrow until you hit 4. Draw a circle around 4 and the smallest number to its right that is larger (5). Swap them, then draw a "reverse" arc over the remaining tail.
Opt Complexity Visuals: Two linear scans of the digits. $O(Log N)$ where N is the value (number of digits).
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a straight line of digits with two or three pointers moving across it.
Mem Vis (BF): An array/list to store all D! permutations.
Mem Vis (Opt): A small array of digits. $O(Log N)$ space.
