ID: 1465
Problem Title: Maximum Area of a Piece of Cake After Horizontal and Vertical Cuts
Brute Force Method: Calculate the area of every single piece of cake created by the intersections of all horizontal and vertical cuts. Find the max.
BF Complexity Visuals: Grid Exhaustion
How to Draw BF: Draw a rectangle with all cuts. Draw a loop that calculates the area of every little square inside the grid. Label $O(H_\text{cuts} \cdot V_\text{cuts})$.
Optimised Pattern: Greedy (Independent Max Gaps)
Opt Vis. Technique: Orthogonal Max-Gap Projection
Dry Run (Opt): The maximum area is simply (Maximum horizontal gap) x (Maximum vertical gap). Sort the cut arrays, find the max difference between adjacent cuts, and multiply.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw the cake. Highlight the largest gap between horizontal lines. Highlight the largest gap between vertical lines. The intersection of these two specific gaps is the largest piece.
Opt Complexity Visuals: Sort-Centric Bottleneck
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw Sort(H) rightarrow Scan(H) rightarrow Sort(V) rightarrow Scan(V). Label $O(H \log H + V \log V)$.
Mem Vis (BF): N/A
Mem Vis (Opt): Two sorted arrays of cuts. Space $O(H + V)$.
