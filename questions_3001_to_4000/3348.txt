Problem Title: Smallest Divisible Digit Product II
Brute Force Method: Check every string lexicographically larger than num, calculate its digit product, and check divisibility by t.
BF Complexity Visuals: Lexicographical Tree Path
How to Draw BF: Draw a tree of all possible numbers. Start from num and follow every right-hand branch. The tree is effectively infinite.
Optimised Pattern: Greedy + Backtracking + Prime Factorization
Opt Vis. Technique: Factor-Filling Slots
Dry Run (Opt): Factorize t into 2s, 3s, 5s, and 7s. Starting from the end of num, try to increment a digit and fill the remaining suffixes with the minimum digits needed to satisfy the remaining factors of t.
How to Draw (Opt): Write num. At a certain digit, change it to something higher. Calculate the "missing" factors. Draw empty slots and fill them with the smallest digits (like 9s then 8s) that cover those factors.
Opt Complexity Visuals: Depth-Limited Backtrack
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a tree with depth N. Show the search "pruning" early when factors cannot be satisfied. $O(N x \text{const})$.
Mem Vis (BF): N/A.
Mem Vis (Opt): A recursive stack of depth N, plus a small frequency array for prime factors of t.
