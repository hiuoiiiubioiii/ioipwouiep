ID: 589
Problem Title: N-ary Tree Preorder Traversal
Brute Force Method: Convert the tree into an adjacency list first, then perform a recursive traversal.
BF Complexity Visuals: Two-pass process with unnecessary intermediate storage. $O(N)$.
How to Draw BF: Draw a tree. Write it as a list of lists. Then traverse that list.
Optimised Pattern: Recursive DFS or Iterative Stack.
Opt Vis. Technique: A "Deep Dive." You visit the parent, then immediately plunge into the first child, then its first child, until you hit a leaf, then move to the next sibling.
Dry Run (Opt): Recursive: `Visit(root)`. For each `child` in `root.children`, `DFS(child)`. Iterative: Push root to stack. While stack not empty, pop, add to result, and push all children to stack in **reverse** order.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw an N-ary tree. Trace a line starting from the root, going to the leftmost child, then its children, then back up slightly to the next sibling.
Opt Complexity Visuals: Single visit to every node. $O(N)$.
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a tree and follow a single arrow that hugs the "left side" of every branch.
Mem Vis (BF): Storing the adjacency list explicitly.
Mem Vis (Opt): Recursion stack or Iterative stack. $O(H)$ or $O(N)$ in worst case.
