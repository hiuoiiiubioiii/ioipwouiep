Problem Title: Push Dominoes
Brute Force Method: Simulate second-by-second. In each second, look at every domino and decide if it falls based on its neighbors' state from the *previous* second.
BF Complexity Visuals: Time-Step Simulation. $O(N \\text{times} \\text{text}{\text{Time}})$.
How to Draw BF: Draw the dominoes. Draw a new row for T=1, T=2... showing the ripple effect until no more changes occur.
Optimised Pattern: Two-Pass Force Calculation or Two-Pointer Segmenting.
Opt Vis. Technique: "Gravity Fields" from L and R.
Dry Run (Opt): Calculate the "Force" from the nearest 'R' on the left (decays with distance) and 'L' on the right. Sum the forces: positive is 'R', negative is 'L', zero is vertical.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw the dominoes. Draw green arrows pointing right from every 'R' that get smaller. Draw blue arrows pointing left from every 'L'. Subtract the arrow sizes at each dot.
Opt Complexity Visuals: Two-Pass Linear Scan. $O(N)$.
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw two parallel timelines (Left-Pass and Right-Pass) merged into a final result timeline.
Mem Vis (BF): Grid of Time-Steps: Draw multiple N-sized arrays for each second of simulation.
Mem Vis (Opt): One Force Array: Draw a single 1D strip of N integers being updated.
