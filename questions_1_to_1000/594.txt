ID: 594
Problem Title: Longest Harmonious Subsequence
Brute Force Method: Generate every possible subsequence, and for each, check if the max and min values differ by exactly 1.
BF Complexity Visuals: Exponential growth $O(2^N)$.
How to Draw BF: Draw [1,3,2,2,5,2,3,7]. List combos like [1,2,2,2] and [3,2,2,2,3]. Check differences.
Optimised Pattern: Frequency Hash Map.
Opt Vis. Technique: A "Neighbor Search." A harmonious subsequence is just the count of a number `X` plus the count of its neighbor `X+1`. You just need to find which pair of neighbors has the highest combined count.
Dry Run (Opt): Build a frequency map of all numbers. For each key `X`, if `X+1` exists in the map, `max_len = max(max_len, map[X] + map[X+1])`.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw the array. Below it, draw a frequency table: `1:1, 2:3, 3:2, 5:1, 7:1`. Check pairs (1,2), (2,3), (3,4)... Sum the values of the pairs and pick the max.
Opt Complexity Visuals: Two linear passes (one to build map, one to check). $O(N)$.
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a horizontal line of numbers flowing into a table, followed by a simple pair-wise scan of that table.
Mem Vis (BF): Recursive stack for subsequence generation.
Mem Vis (Opt): A Hash Map storing unique numbers and their counts. $O(N)$.
