Problem Title: Find Median Given Frequency of Numbers (SQL)
Brute Force Method: Expand the frequency table into a full list of individual numbers (e.g., 1:3 becomes 1,1,1), sort them, and pick the middle index.
BF Complexity Visuals: Memory explosion for large frequencies. $O(\\text{sum Frequency})$.
How to Draw BF: Draw a small table: (Val 10, Freq 1000). Draw 1000 tiny dots to show the waste of space.
Optimised Pattern: Running Total (Cumulative Frequency) Analysis.
Opt Vis. Technique: A progress bar. The median is the point where the progress bar passes the 50% mark of the total population.
Dry Run (Opt): Calculate total count N. Use a window function for cumulative frequency. The median exists where `cumulative_freq - frequency &lt;= N/2` AND `cumulative_freq &gt;= N/2`.
How to Draw (Opt): Draw a table with columns: Value, Freq, and CumSum. Total items = 10. Draw a line at 5 and 6. See which "Value" row those numbers fall into.
Opt Complexity Visuals: Single pass with window functions. $O(N \log N)$ (due to sort).
How to Draw Opt Complexity: Draw a staircase graph where each step's width is the frequency; the median is the step at the midpoint of the X-axis.
Mem Vis (BF): Massive array holding every single occurrence of every number.
Mem Vis (Opt): $O(N)$ space for the cumulative sum table.
